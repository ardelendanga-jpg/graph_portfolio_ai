Tackling Treatment Inertia in HIV & Hypertension Care

People living with HIV on suppressed ART can have better blood pressure and glucose control than HIV-negative adults — yet clinicians often lack access to this evidence. This platform delivers two practical, zero-cost tools to bridge the knowledge gap at the point of care.

⚠

The Problem — Treatment Inertia

Clinicians in busy Southern African settings face a dual epidemic — managing HIV alongside rapidly rising rates of hypertension and diabetes in the same patients. Complex SOPs in lengthy PDFs are rarely consulted during consultations, and there is no structured mechanism to keep frontline staff updated with the latest evidence.

✓

Our Two-Part Solution

Solution 1 — Visual Abstracts: SOPs and landmark research papers are distilled into rich, colour-coded, one-page visual summaries viewable in under two minutes, designed to sit directly within the EMR.   Solution 2 — Automated Literature Digest: PubMed is searched monthly by an automated pipeline; AI generates plain-language summaries contextualised for Southern African practice and delivers them to clinicians' inboxes on the 1st of each month — at zero cost.

HIV + HTN J Int AIDS Soc · 2026

Longitudinal care cascade for hypertension in people with and without HIV in South Africa

This clinic-based cohort study tracks hypertension care engagement over 24 months in South African primary care facilities serving both PLHIV and HIV-negative patients. PLHIV enrolled in ART programmes were significantly more likely to have BP measured, receive a hypertension diagnosis, and be initiated on antihypertensive therapy. Clinical takeaway: ART clinics present an underutilised platform for hypertension integration — consider routine BP measurement at every ART visit without waiting for the patient to report symptoms.

Gumede S, Manne-Goehler J, Oladimeji E et al.
ART + Cardiovascular JAIDS · 2026

Risk of Major Adverse Cardiovascular Events With Dolutegravir-Based vs Efavirenz-Based ART

A comparative cohort study across sub-Saharan African clinics examining MACE outcomes in PLHIV on DTG- versus EFV-based first-line regimens. DTG showed no increased cardiovascular risk compared to EFV, while achieving superior viral suppression rates at 12 and 24 months follow-up. Clinical takeaway: Modern DTG-based first-line ART is both virologically superior and cardiovascularly safe — clinicians need not add cardiovascular monitoring burden when switching patients to DTG.

Siedner MJ, Bärnighausen T, Orrell C et al.
Integration · Zimbabwe Lancet Global Health · 2026

Nurse-led hypertension management integrated into HIV clinics in rural Zimbabwe: cluster RCT

Cluster randomised trial testing a nurse-led, protocol-driven hypertension programme integrated into existing HIV clinics across five rural Zimbabwean districts. The intervention achieved significant improvements in BP control at 12 months (62% vs 34% controlled, p<0.001) with no adverse impact on HIV outcomes. Clinical takeaway: Nurse-led integrated HTN management in HIV clinics is feasible and highly effective in rural Zimbabwe — a model directly replicable at Newlands Clinic with existing staff and infrastructure.

Musuka G, Murewanhema G, Dzinamarira T et al.

SOP & Clinical Guidelines

Newlands Clinic standard operating procedures and key evidence-based reference points

SOP v1.24 · Updated March 2026

Management of Hypertension at Newlands Clinic

BP measurement & classification (Normal → Stage 3), diagnosis criteria, baseline investigations by age group, non-pharmacological management (DASH, exercise, sodium), pharmacological step-up therapy, hypertensive crises (urgency vs emergency), special populations (CKD, pregnancy, elderly >80), follow-up schedule and 6 auditable quality indicators.

References: EDLIZ 2020 · SA National HTN Guidelines 2021 · AHA 2018 · ESC/ESH 2018
Research Evidence · PMID 30916897 · Open Access

HTN & Diabetes in PLHIV: HAALSI Cohort Evidence

Population-based cohort (n=5,059 adults >40 years, rural South Africa, Nov 2014–2015). PLHIV on ART with undetectable viral load: 18% greater HTN awareness (aRR 1.18, CI 1.09–1.28), 24% greater HTN treatment uptake (aRR 1.24, CI 1.10–1.41), lower mean SBP (−5.98 mmHg) and lower blood glucose (−3.77 mmol/L) vs HIV-negative adults.

Journal: J Int AIDS Soc 2019;22(3):e25213 · PMCID: PMC6436499 · Free full text

Key Clinical Points for Frontline Clinicians

💊

Stage 1 HTN (140–159 / 90–99 mmHg)

Start monotherapy. First choice: Amlodipine 10 mg od (CCB) for most. ACE/ARB preferred if DM or CKD present.

💊💊

Stage 2 & 3 HTN (≥160 / ≥100 mmHg)

Start dual therapy immediately: Amlodipine 10 mg + Losartan 50 mg od. Weekly BP checks until controlled.

🚨

Hypertensive Urgency

SBP >160 / DBP >110, no end-organ damage: stat Nifedipine 40 mg po. Recheck 1 hour. Refer if still elevated.

🔬

ART as NCD Platform

PLHIV on suppressed ART may have lower SBP and glucose than HIV-negative peers. Use every ART visit for NCD screening.

⛔

Do NOT Combine

Never co-prescribe ACE inhibitor (Enalapril) + ARB (Losartan). Choose one class — combining causes dangerous hyperkalaemia.

📅

Follow-Up Schedule

New / poorly controlled: weekly BP + monthly review. Controlled ≥1 year: 6-monthly supply, sync with ART refill visits.

How It Works — System Architecture

The complete automated pipeline from PubMed database to clinician inbox, and how visual abstracts are generated

1

GitHub Actions Scheduler — Triggers on 1st of Every Month

A YAML workflow file in the repository (.github/workflows/monthly_digest.yml) fires the Python script at 07:00 UTC on the 1st of each month using a cron trigger. The entire workflow runs in a free GitHub-hosted Ubuntu runner — no servers to manage or pay for.

GitHub Actions cron: '0 7 1 * *' Ubuntu runner Free tier
2

PubMed E-Utilities API — Fetches Latest Articles

The Python script queries the free PubMed API (NCBI E-Utilities) using 3 targeted search terms. It searches the last 35 days, deduplicates results, fetches titles, abstracts, authors and journal details for the top 10 unique articles. Rate limiting is handled with 1-second delays between requests.

NCBI E-Utilities (free) esearch + efetch XML parsing Deduplication
3

Gemini 1.5 Flash — Generates Plain-Language Summaries

Each abstract is sent to Google's Gemini API with a carefully crafted prompt requesting a 3–5 sentence plain-language summary plus a "Clinical takeaway:" line specifically contextualised for Southern African clinical practice. The free tier allows 1,500 requests/day — the digest uses only 10.

Gemini 1.5 Flash Google AI Studio (free) Prompt engineering JSON response parsing
4

Gmail SMTP — Formatted HTML Digest Delivered to Inbox

A professionally formatted HTML email is assembled with article cards, AI summaries, and "Read on PubMed" links. It is sent via Gmail SMTP using an App Password (16-character code generated in Google Account security settings, stored as a GitHub Secret). Works for unlimited recipients.

Gmail SMTP (free) App Password auth HTML email template GitHub Secrets
5

Visual Abstracts — AI-Powered SVG Generation

SOP documents and PubMed articles are fed to Claude AI, which reads the full content, extracts all key clinical data and statistics, then generates rich landscape SVG visual abstracts. Research paper abstracts follow IMRaD structure (Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion). These SVGs are embedded directly in this training hub.

Claude AI (Anthropic) SVG generation IMRaD structure Point-of-care design
6

This Training Hub — Hosted on GitHub Pages (Free)

This website is a single-file HTML application that can be hosted for free on GitHub Pages, or embedded as an iframe within the clinic's existing EMR system. No backend server, no database, no maintenance. All content is hardcoded or loaded from public APIs.

Static HTML/CSS/JS GitHub Pages (free) EMR iframe-embeddable No backend needed
