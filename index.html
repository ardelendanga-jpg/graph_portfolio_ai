Professional Summary

I am an early-career epidemiologist with extensive experience managing and analyzing large-scale, multi-country population health data across Sub-Saharan Africa. My expertise lies in HIV and comorbidities research, including cervical cancer and arterial hypertension.

Currently, I am a Doctoral Student at the University of Bern and a Research Coordinator at Newlands Clinic in Zimbabwe. I hold a Master of Science in Epidemiology from the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine.

My work involves securing research funding, coordinating multi-institutional collaborations, and influencing clinical guidelines. I am passionate about leveraging data to improve health outcomes and am now exploring the intersection of Generative AI and research productivity.

About The Course

Generative AI for Work & Research Productivity is an intensive 8-week program delivered by The GRAPH Courses (University of Geneva). It empowers professionals to use AI tools intelligently for workflow automation, data analysis, and research synthesis.

Course Focus

The curriculum covers the practical application of Large Language Models (LLMs) to automate repetitive tasks, analyze complex datasets without code, and synthesize vast amounts of research literature efficiently.

Key Skills Developed

  • No-Code Data Analysis
  • Content Creation
  • Research Synthesis
